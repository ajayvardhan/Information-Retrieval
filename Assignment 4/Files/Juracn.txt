guabancex was the personification of destruction a trait that the tano associated with the catastrophic nature of atlantic hurricanes  according to tano mythology the zemi of guabancex was entrusted to the ruler of a mystical land aumatex this granted her the title of of the wind but it also imposed the responsibility of repeatedly appeasing the goddess throughout his long reign furthermore due to the importance of the wind for travel between island and the need of good weather imperative for a successful crop other caciques would offer her part of their food during the chohoba ceremony however given guabancexs volatile temper these efforts often failed when they did she would leave his domain enraged and with the intent of bringing destruction to all in her path unleashing the juracnes she began by interrupting the balance established by boinayel and marohu the deities of rain and drought by rotating her arms in a spiral guabancex would pick the water of the ocean and land placing it under the command of coatrisquie who violently forced it back over the tano settlements destroying their bohios and crops she would threaten the other deities in an attempt to have them join the chaos she was always preceded by guataub who heralded her eventual arrival with clouds lightning and thunder the easternmost of the greater antilles puerto rico is often in the path of the north atlantic tropical storms and hurricanes which tend to come ashore on the east coast the tano believed that upon reaching the rainforest peak of el yunque the goddess and her cohorts would clash with their supreme deity ycahu who was believed to live there guabancex has an unspecified connection to caorao a deity that was also associated with storms and that was said to bring them forth by
