includes slow-bouncing keyboard tones and drums the song was written by mendler and american songwriters emanuel kiriakou evan kidd bogart and andrew goldstein and produced by kiriakou and goldstein lyrically the song gives ode to a disaster love and reconciliation and perseverance in love mendlers vocals span from the low note of F3 to the high note of F5.5 in interview for coup de main magazine mendler commented about recording a rap song for the first time she said  i loved recording you know the rap in that was pretty fun it was very experimental for me i havent done much rapping before it was not a deliberate decision we had written the song and i had come in to record it and then the guys were like dont we just try rapping this part so i did and then it kind of just worked and we decided to keep it so not intentional but sometimes things just happen in the moment.6  critical receptionedit  has been compared to the works of cher lloyd and lily allen  the song has received
