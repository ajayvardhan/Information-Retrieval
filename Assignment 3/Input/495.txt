<p>An <b>anticyclonic storm</b> is a weather <a href="/wiki/Storm" title="Storm">storm</a> where winds around the storm flow in the direction opposite to that of the flow about a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">region of low pressure</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anticyclonic_storm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a>, anticyclonic storms involve clockwise wind flow; in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">Southern Hemisphere</a>, they involve counterclockwise wind flow.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anticyclonic" title="Anticyclonic">Anticyclonic</a> storms usually form around <a href="/wiki/Pressure_system" title="Pressure system">high-pressure systems</a>. These do not "contradict" the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coriolis_effect" title="Coriolis effect">Coriolis effect</a>; it predicts such anticyclonic flow about high-pressure regions. Anticyclonic storms, as high-pressure systems, usually accompany cold weather and are frequently a factor in large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snowstorm" title="Snowstorm">snowstorms</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">Jupiter's</a> <a href="/wiki/Great_Red_Spot" title="Great Red Spot">Great Red Spot</a> is a well-known extraterrestrial example of an anticyclonic system.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Anticyclonic_tornado" title="Anticyclonic tornado">Anticyclonic tornadoes</a> often occur;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> while tornadoes' <a href="/wiki/Vortex" title="Vortex">vortices</a> are low-pressure regions, this occurs because tornadoes occur on a small enough scale such that the Coriolis effect is negligible.</p>
