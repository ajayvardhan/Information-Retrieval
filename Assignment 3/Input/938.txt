<p><b>Expedition 25</b> was the 25th long-duration mission to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> (ISS). Expedition 25 began with the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-18" title="Soyuz TMA-18">Soyuz TMA-18</a> undocking on 25 September 2010. Three new crewmembers (Scott Kelly, Alexander Kaleri and Oleg Skripochka) arrived aboard the ISS 10 October 2010 on <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-01M" title="Soyuz TMA-01M">Soyuz TMA-01M</a> to join Douglas Wheelock, Fyodor Yurchikhin and Shannon Walker, and formed the full six member crew of Expedition 25.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> NASA astronaut Doug Wheelock accepted command of Expedition 25 on 22 September 2010, taking over from Russia's <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Skvortsov_(cosmonaut)" title="Aleksandr Skvortsov (cosmonaut)">Aleksandr Skvortsov</a>. The departure of Wheelock, Walker and Yurchikhin on 25 November 2010 marked the official end of Expedition 25.</p>
<p>During Expedition 25 <a href="/wiki/Progress_M-08M" title="Progress M-08M">Progress M-08M</a> spacecraft visited the ISS, docking with the space station on 30 October 2010 bringing 2.5 tons of cargo supplies. Space shuttle Discovery on <a href="/wiki/STS-133" title="STS-133">STS-133</a> mission was scheduled to arrive at the ISS on 3 November 2010 but was rescheduled for launch on 3 February 2011. The 10th anniversary of human life, work and research on the ISS fell during Expedition 25. On 2 November 2000, <a href="/wiki/Expedition_1" title="Expedition 1">Expedition 1</a> Commander <a href="/wiki/William_Shepherd" title="William Shepherd">William Shepherd</a> and Flight Engineers <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Krikalev" title="Sergei Krikalev">Sergei Krikalev</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Gidzenko" title="Yuri Gidzenko">Yuri Gidzenko</a> became the first residents of the space station. Expedition 25 ended on 26 November.</p>

<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a> in Kazakhstan, Expedition 25 Soyuz Commander Alexander Kaleri, NASA Flight Engineer Scott Kelly and Russian Flight Engineer Oleg Skripochka participated in a variety of activities from 26 September to 4 October 2010 as they prepared for their launch on 8 October 2010 (7 October 2010 U.S. Eastern time) in their <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-01M" title="Soyuz TMA-01M">Soyuz TMA-01M</a> spacecraft to the International Space Station. The footage includes the crew's arrival in Baikonur, their suited and unsuited fit checks in their Soyuz spacecraft, the raising of flags outside their Cosmonaut Hotel crew quarters and other traditional activities. The Soyuz TMA-01M spacecraft was mated to its booster in a processing facility for its rollout to the launch pad in Baikonur 5 October 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spacearium_YT1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spacearium_YT1-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spacearium_YT2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spacearium_YT2-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Soyuz TMA-01M spacecraft and booster rocket were moved to Launch Complex 5 (Complex 17P32-5) at Baikonur Cosmodrome on a rail-car 5 October 2010 for final preparations prior to launch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spacearium_YT3_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spacearium_YT3-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spacearium_YT4_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spacearium_YT4-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Progress_M-05M_undocking">Progress M-05M undocking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_25&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Progress M-05M undocking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Russian resupply spacecraft <a href="/wiki/Progress_M-05M" title="Progress M-05M">Progress M-05M</a>, which came to the station in May 2010, was undocked on 25 October 2010 to make room for another resupply spacecraft – <a href="/wiki/Progress_M-08M" title="Progress M-08M">Progress M-08M</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Progress_M-08M">Progress M-08M</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_25&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Progress M-08M">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Progress_M-08M" title="Progress M-08M">Progress M-08M</a> spacecraft delivered about 2.5 tons of cargo supplies including water, air, fuel and hardware for the Russian Molniya-Gamma and Coulomb Crystal experiments to the station.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Soyuz-U" title="Soyuz-U">Soyuz-U</a> carrier rocket with Progress M-08M, identified by NASA as Progress 40 or 40P, was launched from the Baikonur's Gagarin's launch pad at 15:11:50 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a> on 27 October 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> After three days of autonomous flight, at 16:36 UTC on 30 October 2010 Progress M-08M docked with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pirs_docking_compartment" title="Pirs docking compartment">Pirs module</a> nadir port.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> A problem during Progress' approach to the space station forced cosmonauts on the station to intervene. During station-keeping as part of the rendezvous operations, flight controllers in Moscow instructed cosmonaut Alexander Kaleri to activate the <a href="/wiki/TORU" title="TORU">TORU</a> manual docking equipment and take over the piloting tasks from the Progress' autonomous KURS system. The switch to manual mode was decided at range of 194 m. Kaleri worked inside the space station's <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">Zvezda module</a> to fly Progress M-08M remotely using television views and a pair of joysticks and guided it to the successful docking.</p>
