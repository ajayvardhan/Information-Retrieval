<p>The <b>University of Hawaiʻi system</b> (formally the <b>University of Hawaiʻi</b> and popularly known as <b>U.H.</b>) is a public, co-educational college and <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> system that confers associate, bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees through three university campuses, seven <a href="/wiki/Community_college" title="Community college">community college</a> campuses, an employment training center, three university centers, four education centers and various other research facilities distributed across six islands throughout the <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">State of Hawaii</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. All schools of the University of Hawaii system are accredited by the <a href="/wiki/Western_Association_of_Schools_and_Colleges" title="Western Association of Schools and Colleges">Western Association of Schools and Colleges</a>. The U.H. system's main administrative offices are located on the property of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Hawai%CA%BBi_at_M%C4%81noa" title="University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa">University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honolulu,_Hawaii" title="Honolulu, Hawaii">Honolulu CDP</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Colleges_and_universities">Colleges and universities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Hawaii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Colleges and universities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Hawaii,_Manoa" title="University of Hawaii, Manoa">University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa</a> is the flagship institution of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Hawai%CA%BBi" title="University of Hawaiʻi">University of Hawaiʻi</a> system. It was founded as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Land-grant_college" title="Land-grant college">land-grant college</a> under the terms of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Morrill_Land-Grant_Colleges_Act" title="Morrill Land-Grant Colleges Act">Morrill Acts of 1862 and 1890</a>. It is well respected for its programs in <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaiian</a>/<a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Studies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">Astronomy</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_Asian" title="East Asian">East Asian</a> Languages and Literature, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian Studies</a>, Comparative <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">Philosophy</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_Science" title="Marine Science">Marine Science</a>, Second Language Studies, along with <a href="/wiki/Botany" title="Botany">Botany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">Engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethnomusicology" title="Ethnomusicology">Ethnomusicology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geophysics" title="Geophysics">Geophysics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">Law</a>, <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">Business</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linguistics" title="Linguistics">Linguistics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">Mathematics</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">Medicine</a>. The second-largest institution is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Hawaii,_Hilo" title="University of Hawaii, Hilo">University of Hawaiʻi at Hilo</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Hawaii_(island)" title="Hawaii (island)">"Big Island" of Hawaiʻi</a>, with over 3,000 students. The smaller <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Hawaii,_West_Oahu" title="University of Hawaii, West Oahu">University of Hawaiʻi-West Oʻahu</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kapolei" title="Kapolei">Kapolei</a> primarily serves students who reside on <a href="/wiki/Honolulu" title="Honolulu">Honolulu's</a> western and central suburban communities. The University of Hawaiʻi Community College system comprises four <a href="/wiki/Community_college" title="Community college">community colleges</a> island campuses on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/O%27ahu" title="O'ahu">O'ahu</a> and one each on <a href="/wiki/Maui" title="Maui">Maui</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaua%CA%BBi" title="Kauaʻi">Kauaʻi</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hawai%CA%BBi" title="Hawaiʻi">Hawaiʻi</a>. The schools were created to improve accessibility of courses to more Hawaiʻi residents and provide an affordable means of easing the transition from <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_school" title="High school">high school</a> to <a href="/wiki/College" title="College">college</a> for many students. University of Hawaiʻi education centers are located in more remote areas of the State and its several islands, supporting rural communities via distance education.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Board_of_Regents">Board of Regents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Hawaii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Board of Regents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In accordance with Article X, Section 6 of the Hawaiʻi State Constitution, the University of Hawaiʻi system is governed by a Board of Regents, composed of 15 unpaid members who are nominated by a Regents Candidate Advisory Council, appointed by the governor, and confirmed by the state legislature. The Board oversees all aspects of governance for the university system, including its internal structure and management. The board also appoints, evaluates, and if necessary removes the President of the University of Hawaiʻi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_regents">Student regents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Hawaii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Student regents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The University's governing board includes a current student appointed by the Governor of Hawaiʻi to serve a two-year term as a full voting regent. The practice of appointing a student to the Board was approved by the Hawaiʻi State Legislature in 1997.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_alumni">Notable alumni</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Hawaii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Notable alumni">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_University_of_Hawaii_alumni" title="List of University of Hawaii alumni">List of University of Hawaii alumni</a></div>
<p>Alumni of the University of Hawaiʻi system include many notable persons in various walks of life. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Inouye" title="Daniel Inouye">Senator Daniel Inouye</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tammy_Duckworth" title="Tammy Duckworth">Tammy Duckworth</a> both are veterans of the US military who were injured during in the line of duty then later entered government service. <a href="/wiki/Bette_Midler" title="Bette Midler">Bette Midler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georgia_Engel" title="Georgia Engel">Georgia Engel</a> are successful entertainers on the national stage. <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">President Barack Obama's</a> parents, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barack_Obama,_Sr." title="Barack Obama, Sr.">Barack Obama, Sr.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ann_Dunham" title="Ann Dunham">S. Ann Dunham</a>, and half-sister, <a href="/wiki/Maya_Soetoro-Ng" title="Maya Soetoro-Ng">Maya Soetoro-Ng</a>, also earned degrees from the Mānoa campus, where his parents met in a Russian language class. His mother earned three degrees from the University of Hawaiʻi including a Ph.D. in anthropology.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_faculty">Notable faculty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Hawaii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Notable faculty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_University_of_Hawaii_faculty" title="List of University of Hawaii faculty">List of University of Hawaii faculty</a></div>
<p>The University of Hawaiʻi system has had many faculty members of note. Many were visiting faculty or came after they won major awards like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nobel_Laureate" title="Nobel Laureate">Nobel Laureate</a> <a href="/wiki/Georg_von_B%C3%A9k%C3%A9sy" title="Georg von Békésy">Dr. Georg von Békésy</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ryuzo_Yanagimachi" title="Ryuzo Yanagimachi">Dr. Ryuzo Yanagimachi</a>, principal investigator of the research group that developed a method of cloning from adult animal cells, is still on the faculty.</p>
