<p><b>Alyssa Ashley Reid</b> (born March 15, 1993) is a Canadian singer-songwriter. Reid rose to fame in 2011, following the release of her single "<a href="/wiki/Alone_Again_(Alyssa_Reid_song)" title="Alone Again (Alyssa Reid song)">Alone Again</a>".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyssa_Reid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Edmonton" title="Edmonton">Edmonton</a>, Alberta and raised in <a href="/wiki/Brampton" title="Brampton">Brampton</a>, Ontario, Reid's family moved around Canada often. Reid stated that it gave her the life experience she needed to enter the music industry. She wrote her first song at the age of 7 and started vocal lessons at the age of 9. From then on, she has continued to develop her voice into what has become a singular vocal style. She is of Irish and Polish<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mindyourmind_interview_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-mindyourmind_interview-1">[1]</a></sup> heritage.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyssa_Reid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id='2010–2011:_Career_beginnings_and_"The_Game"'></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.932011:_Career_beginnings_and_.22The_Game.22">2010–2011: Career beginnings and "The Game"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyssa_Reid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title='Edit section: 2010–2011: Career beginnings and "The Game"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Reid was soon discovered by writer, producer, and Wax Records co-founder Jamie Appleby, who signed her to his label after finding a parody of Justin Bieber's "One Less Lonely Girl" (titled "One Less Lonely Boy") that she had posted on YouTube, which went viral.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her single "<a href="/wiki/Alone_Again_(Alyssa_Reid_song)" title="Alone Again (Alyssa Reid song)">Alone Again</a>" featuring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P._Reign" title="P. Reign">P. Reign</a>, released in November 2010, uses lyrics from Heart's 1987 hit "<a href="/wiki/Alone_(Heart_song)" title="Alone (Heart song)">Alone</a>", and was co-written with Wax Records label owner Jamie Appleby, with the original 'Heart' writers Billy Steinberg and Tom Kelly. The song entered the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> at #79 for the week of January 1, 2011 and peaked at #11 the week of April 23, 2011. As of the week of September 24, 2011, the single has spent 39 weeks on the chart. It went on to be CRIA certified double platinum in Canada and sold over a million copies around the world.</p>
<p>In February 2011 she reached #1 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard's</a> "Canadian Emerging Artist" chart and held there for a record breaking 33 weeks straight. Her single "<a href="/wiki/The_Game_(Alyssa_Reid_song)" title="The Game (Alyssa Reid song)">The Game</a>" was released on May 31, 2011 and entered the Canadian Hot 100 at #89 for the week of August 3, 2011, peaking at #35 as of the week of September 24, 2011. In February 2012, "Alone Again" achieved major success in the United Kingdom, where it peaked at number two on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>, beaten to the summit by "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Titanium_(David_Guetta_song)" title="Titanium (David Guetta song)">Titanium</a>" by <a href="/wiki/David_Guetta" title="David Guetta">David Guetta</a> featuring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sia_Furler" title="Sia Furler">Sia</a>.</p>
<p>On April 27, 2011 she performed her debuted track "Alone Again" at Chester University, along with several other covers such as "Gotye".</p>
<p>Reid was nominated for a 2011 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MuchMusic" title="MuchMusic">MuchMusic</a> Video Award for Pop Video of the Year for "Alone Again". Her first ever visit to the UK involved a series of interviews with BBC Radio One, Capital FM and a live interview with Fame Factor TV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>The Game</i> was her debut studio album, recorded in 2010 and released on June 21, 2011 by Wax Records. Reid worked with composers and producers such as Billy Steinberg and Jamie Appleby. The album was released in the US as a digital download on May 7, 2013.</p>
<p>On July 20, 2011 Reid performed at The Baths Hall in <a href="/wiki/Scunthorpe" title="Scunthorpe">Scunthorpe</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> along with other acts for the Scunthorpe Telegraph's 75th Birthday and Lincs FM's 20th Birthday.</p>
<p>Reid performed at the 2012 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juno_Awards" title="Juno Awards">Juno Awards</a> on April 1, 2012, and was nominated in the "Best New Artist" category.</p>
<h3><span id="2011–present:_Touring,_Time_Bomb,_and_other_albums"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.93present:_Touring.2C_Time_Bomb.2C_and_other_albums">2011–present: Touring, <i>Time Bomb</i>, and other albums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyssa_Reid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2011–present: Touring, Time Bomb, and other albums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 1, 2011, Alyssa Reid began touring with <a href="/wiki/Neverest" title="Neverest">Neverest</a> for the Canadian Tour 2011. Reid started her first UK Tour in April 2012 by promoting her single "Alone Again". During this time, Reid was also featured on Wax Records label-mate <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Labelle" title="Jesse Labelle">Jesse Labelle</a>'s 2012 single "<a href="/wiki/Heartbreak_Coverup" title="Heartbreak Coverup">Heartbreak Coverup</a>".</p>
<p>Reid, along with <a href="/wiki/Danny_Fernandes" title="Danny Fernandes">Danny Fernandes</a> and <a href="/wiki/JRDN" title="JRDN">JRDN</a>, joined <a href="/wiki/Hedley_(band)" title="Hedley (band)">Hedley</a> on their Wild Live 2014 Canadian Tour, which began on February 14, 2014.</p>
<p>On February 11, 2014, her second studio album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Time_Bomb_(Alyssa_Reid_album)" title="Time Bomb (Alyssa Reid album)">Time Bomb</a></i> was released. It featured "Hurricane" (released in the same year), and two other singles ("Running Guns" and "Satisfaction Guaranteed") that had been released a year earlier. Reid toured across Canada alongside label-mate <a href="/wiki/Virginia_to_Vegas" title="Virginia to Vegas">Virginia to Vegas</a> for her "The Time Bomb Tour", which began October 16, 2014.</p>
<p>On July 10, 2015, Reid released a new single titled "Dangerous", featuring The Heist. She released "Tomorrow" on November 2 of the same year. Reid's third album <i>Phoenix</i> was released on November 27, 2015.</p>
<p>On June 6, 2016, Alyssa Reid announced that her single "Rollercoaster" would be released on June 17, and will be featured on her upcoming fourth album.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alyssa_Reid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reid resides in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, Ontario. She went to St. Thomas Aquinas Catholic School for grades 9 to 11, and then attended Chinguacousy Secondary School through to graduation.</p>
