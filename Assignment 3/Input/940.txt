<p><b>Expedition 27</b> was the 27th long-duration expedition to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> (ISS), starting on 16 March 2011. Expedition 27 saw numerous notable events, including the undocking of the <a href="/wiki/Progress_M-09M" title="Progress M-09M">Progress M-09M</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kounotori_2" title="Kounotori 2">Kounotori 2</a> spacecraft, the arrival of the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-21" title="Soyuz TMA-21">Soyuz TMA-21</a> and <a href="/wiki/Progress_M-10M" title="Progress M-10M">Progress M-10M</a> spacecraft, and the final rendezvous with the ISS of <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">Space Shuttle <i>Endeavour</i></a>, on its last mission, <a href="/wiki/STS-134" title="STS-134">STS-134</a>. The expedition ended on 23 May 2011 with the departure of the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-20" title="Soyuz TMA-20">Soyuz TMA-20</a> spacecraft, although command of the station was ceremonially handed over to the crew of <a href="/wiki/Expedition_28" title="Expedition 28">Expedition 28</a> on 22 May.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kounotori_2_undocking">Kounotori 2 undocking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_27&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Kounotori 2 undocking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After an extended two-month stay, <a href="/wiki/Kounotori_2" title="Kounotori 2">Kounotori 2</a> was detached from the nadir docking port of the <a href="/wiki/Harmony_(ISS_module)" title="Harmony (ISS module)"><i>Harmony</i></a> module by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadarm_2" title="Canadarm 2">Canadarm 2</a> robotic arm at 15:29 UTC on 28 March 2011, and released at 15:46 UTC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Launched in January 2011 to resupply the ISS, Kounotori 2 (also known as HTV-2) was the second Japanese <a href="/wiki/H-II_Transfer_Vehicle" title="H-II Transfer Vehicle">H-II Transfer Vehicle</a>. It reentered Earth's atmosphere at around 03:09 UTC on 30 March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soyuz_TMA-21_docking">Soyuz TMA-21 docking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_27&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Soyuz TMA-21 docking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-21" title="Soyuz TMA-21">Soyuz TMA-21</a> spacecraft launched from <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a> on 4 April 2011, carrying Expedition 27 crew members Aleksandr Samokutyayev, Andrei Borisenko and Ronald Garan. It docked with the space station on 6 April at 23:09 UTC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The docking to the <a href="/wiki/Poisk_(ISS_module)" title="Poisk (ISS module)"><i>Poisk</i></a> module occurred as the two spacecraft were orbiting over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andes_Mountains" title="Andes Mountains">Andes Mountains</a> in Chile. The hatches between Soyuz TMA-21 and the ISS were opened at 2:13 UTC on 7 April.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The three Soyuz crew members were welcomed aboard for a crew greeting ceremony and a mandatory safety orientation by Expedition 27 Commander Dmitri Kondratyev and Flight Engineers Catherine Coleman and Paolo Nespoli.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="50th_anniversary_of_first_spaceflight">50th anniversary of first spaceflight</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_27&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 50th anniversary of first spaceflight">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 12 April 2011, the Expedition 27 crew recorded a special video message aboard the ISS in celebration of the 50th anniversary of the first manned spaceflight, which was conducted by <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> cosmonaut <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Gagarin" title="Yuri Gagarin">Yuri Gagarin</a> in 1961. The crew recorded their greeting in Russian, English, and Italian while wearing black Gagarin T-shirts.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Progress_M-09M_undocking">Progress M-09M undocking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_27&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Progress M-09M undocking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Progress_M-09M" title="Progress M-09M">Progress M-09M</a> cargo spacecraft was undocked from the station's <a href="/wiki/Pirs_(ISS_module)" title="Pirs (ISS module)"><i>Pirs</i></a> module at 11:41 UTC on 22 April 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> After departing the space station, the spacecraft was used for the Radar-Progress scientific experiment to investigate a reflection feature of the <a href="/wiki/Plasma_(physics)" title="Plasma (physics)">plasma</a> generated by operations of the Progress propulsion system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Upon the completion of this experiment, the spacecraft was deorbited, and reentered over the "<a href="/wiki/Spacecraft_cemetery" title="Spacecraft cemetery">spacecraft cemetery</a>" in the South Pacific Ocean.</p>
<p>Progress M-09M had been launched on a resupply mission to the ISS on 28 January 2011. It carried 2,666 kilograms (5,880 lb) of cargo to the space station, consisting of 1,444 kilograms (3,180 lb) of dry cargo, 752 kilograms (1,660 lb) of propellant, 50 kilograms (110 lb) of <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> and 420 kilograms (930 lb) of water.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Progress_M-10M_docking">Progress M-10M docking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_27&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Progress M-10M docking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Flying two days autonomously after liftoff from the Baikonour Cosmodrome, the <a href="/wiki/Progress_M-10M" title="Progress M-10M">Progress M-10M</a> cargo spacecraft arrived at the ISS on 29 April 2011, successfully docking at 14:19 UTC to the nadir port of the <i><a href="/wiki/Pirs_(ISS_module)" title="Pirs (ISS module)">Pirs</a></i> module, which had been vacated by Progress M-09M.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The docking occurred as the two spacecraft were traveling 220 miles (354 kilometers) over western <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>. The linkup happened just over five hours before NASA's first launch attempt of the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">Space Shuttle <i>Endeavour</i></a> on the <a href="/wiki/STS-134" title="STS-134">STS-134</a> mission. The shuttle launch was scrubbed because of the failure of two heaters on one of <i>Endeavour'</i>s auxiliary power units. <i>Endeavour</i> finally launched successfully on 16 May 2011.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="STS-134">STS-134</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_27&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: STS-134">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/STS-134" title="STS-134">STS-134</a>, the second-to-the-last mission of the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_program" title="Space Shuttle program">Space Shuttle program</a>, was launched from <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">Kennedy Space Center</a> on 16 May 2011 at 12:56 UTC. <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">Space Shuttle <i>Endeavour</i></a> docked to the ISS on 18 May at 10:14 UTC, and delivered the <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Magnetic_Spectrometer" title="Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer">Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer 2</a> (AMS-2) and an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Express_Logistics_Carrier" title="Express Logistics Carrier">Express Logistics Carrier</a> to the station, completing the assembly of the <a href="/wiki/US_Orbital_Segment" title="US Orbital Segment">US Orbital Segment</a> of the ISS. On 22 May, while <i>Endeavour</i> was docked, the members of Expedition 27 formally handed over command of the station to the members of <a href="/wiki/Expedition_28" title="Expedition 28">Expedition 28</a>. The following day, on 23 May, the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-20" title="Soyuz TMA-20">Soyuz TMA-20</a> spacecraft departed from the ISS, returning Expedition 27 crew members Paolo Nespoli, Catherine Coleman and Dmitri Kondratyev to Earth. Soyuz TMA-20 landed safely in central <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> at 02:27 UTC on 24 May 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> having taken photographs of the shuttle docked at the ISS prior to re-entry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
