<p>The <i><b>Monthly Weather Review</b></i> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peer-reviewed" title="Peer-reviewed">peer-reviewed</a> <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">scientific journal</a> published by the <a href="/wiki/American_Meteorological_Society" title="American Meteorological Society">American Meteorological Society</a>. It covers research related to analysis and prediction of observed and modeled circulations of the atmosphere, including technique development, <a href="/wiki/Data_assimilation" title="Data assimilation">data assimilation</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Model_validation" title="Model validation">model validation</a>, and relevant case studies. This includes papers on numerical techniques and data assimilation techniques that apply to the atmosphere and/or ocean environment. The <a href="/wiki/Editor-in-chief" title="Editor-in-chief">editor-in-chief</a> is David M. Schultz (<a href="/wiki/University_of_Manchester" title="University of Manchester">University of Manchester</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monthly_Weather_Review&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The journal was established in July 1872 by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Signal_Corps" title="United States Army Signal Corps">United States Army Signal Corps</a>. It was issued by the Office of the Chief Signal Officer from 1872 until 1891. In 1891, the Signal Office's <a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">meteorological</a> responsibilities were transferred to the Weather Bureau under the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Agriculture" title="United States Department of Agriculture">United States Department of Agriculture</a>. The Weather Bureau published the journal until 1970 when the Bureau became part of the newly formed <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration">National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration</a>, which published it until the end of 1973. Since 1974, it has been published by the <a href="/wiki/American_Meteorological_Society" title="American Meteorological Society">American Meteorological Society</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAA_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NOAA-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Abstracting_and_indexing">Abstracting and indexing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monthly_Weather_Review&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Abstracting and indexing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The journal is abstracted and indexed in <a href="/wiki/Current_Contents" title="Current Contents">Current Contents</a>/Physical, Chemical &amp; Earth Sciences and the <a href="/wiki/Science_Citation_Index" title="Science Citation Index">Science Citation Index</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISI_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ISI-2">[2]</a></sup> According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_Citation_Reports" title="Journal Citation Reports">Journal Citation Reports</a></i>, the journal has a 2012 <a href="/wiki/Impact_factor" title="Impact factor">impact factor</a> of 2.758.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WoS_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-WoS-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
