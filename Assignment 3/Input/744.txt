<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Tongue of the Ocean</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>Tongue of the Ocean</b> (<b>TOTO</b>) is the name of a deep <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_trench" title="Oceanic trench">oceanic trench</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> separating the islands of <a href="/wiki/Andros,_Bahamas" title="Andros, Bahamas">Andros</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Providence" title="New Providence">New Providence</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tongue_of_the_Ocean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The TOTO is a U-shaped, relatively flat-bottomed trench approximately 20 miles (32 km) wide by 150 miles (240 km) long. Its depth varies gradually from 3,600 feet (1,100 m) in the south to 6,600 feet (2,000 m) in the north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Its only exposure to the open ocean is at the northern end. Except for the northern ocean opening, the TOTO is surrounded by numerous islands, reefs, and shoals which make a peripheral shelter isolating it from ocean disturbances, particularly high ambient noise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>This channel and the Providence Channel are the two main branches of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Bahama_Canyon" title="Great Bahama Canyon">Great Bahama Canyon</a>, a submerged geological feature formed by erosion during periods of lower sea level. During their early history the Tongue of the Ocean and the Providence Channels were broad, relatively shallow basins flanked by growing carbonate banks. As the Blake-Bahama platform subsided, sedimentation kept pace with subsidence on the banks, but not in the basins.</p>
