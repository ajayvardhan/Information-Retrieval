<p><b>Yang Liwei</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">杨利伟</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">楊利偉</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Yáng Lìwěi</span></i> ) (born 21 June 1965) is a major general, military pilot, and <a href="/wiki/China_National_Space_Administration" title="China National Space Administration">China National Space Administration</a> <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">astronaut</a>. In 2003, he became the first person sent into space by the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_space_program" title="Chinese space program">Chinese space program</a>. This mission, <a href="/wiki/Shenzhou_5" title="Shenzhou 5">Shenzhou 5</a>, made China the third country to independently send humans into space.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Liwei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yang was born in <a href="/wiki/Suizhong_County" title="Suizhong County">Suizhong County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huludao" title="Huludao">Huludao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a> province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chinadaily-jan24_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-chinadaily-jan24-1">[1]</a></sup> His mother was a teacher, and his father was an accountant at a state agricultural firm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-st-yang_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-st-yang-2">[2]</a></sup> Yang Liwei's wife is also a <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> (PLA) officer, with whom he has a son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xinhua-15oct_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-xinhua-15oct-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1983, he was admitted to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Number_2_PLAAF_Flight_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Number 2 PLAAF Flight Academy (page does not exist)">Number 2 PLAAF Flight Academy</a> (空军第2飞行学院) and graduated four years later. He participated in the screening process for astronauts in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the PLAAF, he logged 1350 hours of flight time as a fighter pilot before he went to space training.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chinadaily-jan24_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-chinadaily-jan24-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spaceflight_career">Spaceflight career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Liwei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Spaceflight career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yang was selected as an astronaut candidate in 1998 and has trained for space flight since then.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-st-yang_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-st-yang-2">[2]</a></sup> He was chosen from the final pool of 13 candidates to fly on China's first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manned_space_mission" title="Manned space mission">manned space mission</a>. A former fighter pilot in the Aviation Military Unit of the PLA, he held the rank of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Colonel" title="Lieutenant Colonel">Lieutenant Colonel</a> at the time of his mission.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-st-yang_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-st-yang-2">[2]</a></sup> He was promoted to full <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">Colonel</a> on 20 October 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xinhua-19oct_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-xinhua-19oct-5">[5]</a></sup> According to the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Youth_Daily" title="Youth Daily">Youth Daily</a></i>, the decision had been made in advance of his spaceflight, but Yang was not made aware of it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xinhua-19oct_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-xinhua-19oct-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was launched into space aboard his <i><a href="/wiki/Shenzhou_5" title="Shenzhou 5">Shenzhou 5</a></i> spacecraft atop a <a href="/wiki/Long_March_2F" title="Long March 2F">Long March 2F</a> rocket from <a href="/wiki/Jiuquan_Satellite_Launch_Center" title="Jiuquan Satellite Launch Center">Jiuquan Satellite Launch Center</a> at 09:00 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_Standard_Time" title="Chinese Standard Time">CST</a> (01:00 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>) on 15 October 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pdaily-15oct_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-pdaily-15oct-6">[6]</a></sup> Prior to his launch almost nothing was made public about the Chinese astronaut candidates; his selection for the <i>Shenzhou 5</i> launch was only leaked to the media one day before the launch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chinadaily-jan24_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-chinadaily-jan24-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yang Liwei has reported the apparition of abnormal vibrations 120 seconds after launch, he described as "very uncomfortable". As a consequence, corrective measures were swiftly taken to the design of the following <a href="/wiki/Long_March_2F" title="Long March 2F">CZ-2F</a> carrier rocket for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shenzhou-6" title="Shenzhou-6">Shenzhou-6</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CZ-2F_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-CZ-2F-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yang punctuated his journey with regular updates on his condition—variations of "I feel good", the last coming as the capsule floated to the ground after re-entry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xinhua-15oct_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-xinhua-15oct-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters-16oct_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-16oct-8">[8]</a></sup> He spoke to his wife as the Shenzhou 5 started its eighth circuit around the Earth, assuring her from space: "I feel very good, don't worry".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xinhua-15oct_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-xinhua-15oct-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters-16oct_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-16oct-8">[8]</a></sup> He ate specially designed packets of shredded pork with garlic, <a href="/wiki/Kung_Pao_chicken" title="Kung Pao chicken">Kung Pao chicken</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eight_treasure" title="Eight treasure">eight treasure</a> rice, along with Chinese herbal <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">tea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xinhua-19oct_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-xinhua-19oct-5">[5]</a></sup> In the middle of the journey, state television broadcast footage of Yang waving a small flag of the People's Republic of China and that of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> inside his capsule.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xinhua-19oct_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-xinhua-19oct-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters-16oct_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-16oct-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>State media said Yang's capsule was supplied with a gun, a knife and tent in case he landed in the wrong place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters-16oct_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-16oct-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yang's craft landed in the grasslands of the Chinese region of <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a> at around 06:30 CST on October 16, 2003 (22:00 UTC), having completed 14 <a href="/wiki/Orbit" title="Orbit">orbits</a> and travelled more than <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1_E8_m" title="1 E8 m">600,000 km</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters-16oct_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-16oct-8">[8]</a></sup> Yang left the capsule about 15 minutes after landing, and was congratulated by <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Premier of the People's Republic of China">Premier</a> <a href="/wiki/Wen_Jiabao" title="Wen Jiabao">Wen Jiabao</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters-16oct_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-16oct-8">[8]</a></sup> But the astronaut's bleeding lips seen in the official images broadcast sparked rumours of a hard landing confirmed by accounts of personnel present at the landing site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although the first Chinese citizen in space, Yang Liwei is not the first person of Chinese origin in space. <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>-born <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Wang" title="Taylor Wang">Taylor Wang</a> flew on <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a> mission <a href="/wiki/STS-51-B" title="STS-51-B">STS-51-B</a> in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp-17oct_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp-17oct-10">[10]</a></sup> Wang, however, had become a United States citizen in 1975.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp-17oct_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp-17oct-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yang visited Hong Kong on 31 October 2003, holding talks and sharing his experiences during a six-day stay in the territory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMCP-visit_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-SMCP-visit-11">[11]</a></sup> The visit coincided with an exhibition that featured his reentry capsule, spacesuit and leftover food from his 21-hour mission. On November 5, he travelled to <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMCP-visit_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-SMCP-visit-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 7 November, Yang received the title of "Space Hero" from <a href="/wiki/Jiang_Zemin" title="Jiang Zemin">Jiang Zemin</a>, the Chairman of the PRC <a href="/wiki/Central_Military_Commission" title="Central Military Commission">Central Military Commission</a> (CMC).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chinadaily-jan24_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-chinadaily-jan24-1">[1]</a></sup> He also received a badge of honour during a ceremony at the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hall_of_the_People" title="Great Hall of the People">Great Hall of the People</a>. Russia awarded him the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gagarin_medal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gagarin medal (page does not exist)">Gagarin medal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Chinese_University_of_Hong_Kong" title="Chinese University of Hong Kong">Chinese University of Hong Kong</a> has given Yang an honorary doctorate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chinadaily-jan24_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-chinadaily-jan24-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Asteroid" title="Asteroid">asteroid</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/21064_Yangliwei" title="21064 Yangliwei">21064 Yangliwei</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fossil_Birds" title="Fossil Birds">fossil bird</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Dalingheornis" title="Dalingheornis">Dalingheornis liweii</a></i> are named after him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chinadaily-jan24_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-chinadaily-jan24-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sinica_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-sinica-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_Shenzhou_5">After Shenzhou 5</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yang_Liwei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: After Shenzhou 5">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a move similar to that taken by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> with national space flight hero <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Gagarin" title="Yuri Gagarin">Yuri Gagarin</a>, an official decision to no longer assign him to spaceflight missions was made.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (July 2015)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Yang was promoted to Major General on 22 July 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Educational,_Scientific_and_Cultural_Organization" title="United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization">United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization</a>(UNESCO) awarded the UNESCO Medal on Space Science to Yang in October 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
