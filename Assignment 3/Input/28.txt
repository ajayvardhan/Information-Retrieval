<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Haboob</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>A <b>haboob</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">هَبوب</span>‎, <small><a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Arabic" title="Romanization of Arabic">translit.</a> </small><i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Arabic transliteration">habūb</span></i>, <small><a href="/wiki/Literal_translation" title="Literal translation">lit.</a> </small>'blasting/drifting'‎) is a type of intense <a href="/wiki/Dust_storm" title="Dust storm">dust storm</a> carried on an atmospheric <a href="/wiki/Gravity_current" title="Gravity current">gravity current</a>, also known as a <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">weather front</a>. Haboobs occur regularly in <a href="/wiki/Arid" title="Arid">arid</a> regions throughout the world.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haboob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During thunderstorm formation, winds move in a direction opposite to the storm's travel, and they move from all directions into the thunderstorm. When the storm collapses, and begins to release precipitation, wind directions reverse, gusting outward from the storm and generally gusting the strongest in the direction of the storm's travel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Haboobs can also form when a strong thunderstorm weakens rapidly, and releases a <a href="/wiki/Microburst" title="Microburst">microburst</a>.</p>
<p>When this downdraft of cold air, or <a href="/wiki/Downburst" title="Downburst">downburst</a>, reaches the ground, it blows dry, loose silt and clay (collectively, dust) up from the desert, creating a wall of sediment that precedes the storm cloud. This wall of dust can be up to 100 km (62 mi) wide and several kilometers in elevation. At their strongest, haboob winds often travel at 35–100 km/h (22–62 mph), and they may approach with little or no warning. Often rain does not appear at ground level as it evaporates in the hot, dry air (a phenomenon known as <a href="/wiki/Virga" title="Virga">virga</a>). The evaporation cools the rushing air even further and accelerates it. Occasionally, when the rain does persist, it can contain a considerable quantity of dust. Severe cases are called <i>mud storms</i>. Eye and respiratory system protection is advisable for anyone who must be outside during a haboob. Moving to shelter is highly advised during a strong event.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Occurrence">Occurrence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haboob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Occurrence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_East">Middle East</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haboob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Middle East">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Haboobs have been observed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sahara_desert" title="Sahara desert">Sahara desert</a> (typically <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>, where they were named and described), as well as across the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a>, throughout <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>, and in the most arid regions of <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Haboob <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">winds</a> in the Arabian Peninsula, Iraq, Kuwait are frequently created by the collapse of a <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorm</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_Africa">North Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haboob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: North Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>African haboobs result from the northward summer shift of the inter-tropical front into <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, bringing moisture from the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haboob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Haboobs in Australia may be frequently associated with <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">cold fronts</a>. The deserts of <a href="/wiki/Central_Australia" title="Central Australia">Central Australia</a>, especially near <a href="/wiki/Alice_Springs" title="Alice Springs">Alice Springs</a>, are particularly prone to haboobs, with sand and debris reaching several kilometers into the sky and leaving up to 30 centimetres (1 ft) of sand in the haboob's path.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_America">North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haboob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/North_American_Monsoon" title="North American Monsoon">North American Monsoon</a></div>
<p>As with haboobs in the Arabian Peninsula, Iraq and Kuwait, haboob occurrences in North America are often created by the collapse of a thunderstorm.</p>
<p>The arid and semiarid regions of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>—in fact, any dry region—may experience haboobs. In North America, the most common terms for these events are either <a href="/wiki/Dust_storm" title="Dust storm">dust storm</a> or sandstorm. In the U.S., they frequently occur in the deserts of <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>, including around the cities of <a href="/wiki/Yuma,_Arizona" title="Yuma, Arizona">Yuma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Albuquerque,_New_Mexico" title="Albuquerque, New Mexico">Albuquerque</a>; in eastern <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> They also sometimes occur in the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Plateau_(ecoregion)" title="Columbia Plateau (ecoregion)">Columbia Basin</a>, of <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Washington" title="Eastern Washington">Eastern</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_(State)" title="Washington (State)">Washington</a>, almost always leading to an impact with the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spokane" title="Spokane">Spokane</a>. If the storms are strong enough, they can reach as far east as the <a href="/wiki/Rathdrum_Prairie" title="Rathdrum Prairie">Rathdrum Prairie</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Palouse" title="Palouse">Palouse</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Idaho" title="Idaho">Idaho</a> <a href="/wiki/Idaho_Panhandle" title="Idaho Panhandle">Panhandle</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mars">Mars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haboob&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Climate_of_Mars#Effect_of_dust_storms" title="Climate of Mars">Global dust storms on Mars</a> have been compared to haboobs on Earth. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
