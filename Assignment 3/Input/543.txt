<p><i><b>Black Scorpion</b></i> is an action-crime TV series that aired on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sci_Fi_Channel_(United_States)" title="Sci Fi Channel (United States)">Sci-Fi Channel</a> in 2001. It aired in Canada on <a href="/wiki/Space_(TV_channel)" title="Space (TV channel)">Space</a>. The series is based on two <a href="/wiki/Roger_Corman" title="Roger Corman">Roger Corman</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Showtime_(cable_network)" title="Showtime (cable network)">Showtime</a> TV-movies: <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Scorpion_(film)" title="Black Scorpion (film)">Black Scorpion</a></i> (1995) and its sequel <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Scorpion_II:_Aftershock" title="Black Scorpion II: Aftershock">Black Scorpion II: Aftershock</a></i> (1997). The show focuses on a female police officer who, by night, takes to the streets and fights crime as the <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">superhero</a> the Black Scorpion.</p>
<p>Episode 1.1, 1.3 and 1.8 were combined into a 2002 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Straight_to_video" title="Straight to video">straight to video</a> movie called <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sting_of_the_Black_Scorpion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sting of the Black Scorpion (page does not exist)">Sting of the Black Scorpion</a></i>.</p>
<p>The series was available on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> in North America.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lead_character">Lead character</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Scorpion_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lead character">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Darcy_Walker">Darcy Walker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Scorpion_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Darcy Walker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Black Scorpion's real name is Darcy Walker, played by <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Lintel" title="Michelle Lintel">Michelle Lintel</a>. She lives in Angel City (a fictionalized version of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles</a>), where she works as a police detective. Darcy's father was shot by the city's crooked district attorney. The D.A. was arrested, but the charges were suspiciously dropped. That was when Darcy decided to take to the streets as the vigilante, Black Scorpion. In the original Corman films, the role of Darcy was played by <a href="/wiki/Joan_Severance" title="Joan Severance">Joan Severance</a>. In the series, she is a red-headed enforcer with a badge who enforces the law more efficiently without the badge as her crime-fighting alter-ego, Black Scorpion. She appears to be a big fan of the color black, a fact she made clear during an interview after being asked with such a trivial question which would explain the color of her costume. However, as shown in the first episode, her choice of the scorpion relates to an old fable about a scorpion trying to cross a river, which her father told her.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Black_Scorpion">Black Scorpion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Scorpion_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Black Scorpion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Apart from being an excellent hand-to-hand fighter and an expert with computers, Black Scorpion has no superpowers. She relies on various specially developed technologies, chief among them the a car called the <i>Scorpionmobile</i> with voice command weaponry and with a disguise capability to make it look like a regular Corvette. She also wears a ring that shoots powerful blasts of energy, and possesses a similar transformation capability to the Scorpionmobile for when Darcy needs to switch into her heroine costume <i>in the field</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_major_characters">Other major characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Scorpion_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other major characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>Detective Steve Rafferty</b>: Darcy's partner and love interest. Apart from Darcy, Steve is the best cop on the force. At present, he is on a mission to unmask the Black Scorpion. Darcy wants to tell Steve that she's the Black Scorpion, but whenever she tries, the opportunity slips away. Steve may also have a crush on the Black Scorpion, which visibly upsets Darcy, forming a bizarre love triangle between Steve, herself, and her costumed alter-ego. He is played by <a href="/wiki/Scott_Valentine_(actor)" title="Scott Valentine (actor)">Scott Valentine</a>. Of special note, Scott Valentine played a cameo in the second Black Scorpion film as minor thug.</li>
<li><b>Specs</b> and <b>Slugger</b>: A pair of incompetent cops that work with Darcy and Steve. Often the pair get into car crashes while chasing Black Scorpion. They are played by Shane Powers and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Steven_Kravitz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Steven Kravitz (page does not exist)">Steven Kravitz</a> respectively. Of note they are the only characters, save Gangster Prankster and Aftershock, to return for the series with their original actors.</li>
<li><b>Argyle Sims</b>: Darcy's personal mechanic, he is the only person whom Darcy allowed to know her identity unconditionally, since he freely gave her most of her more hi-tech equipment. He is somewhat a mentor figure to her at times, but his role is watered down somewhat in the series, only seeming to appear when a new gadget is needed. In one episode, he garbed himself as the "Argyle Avenger", after tipping off Detective Steve Rafferty who couldn't make it in time, in order to aid Darcy (Black Scorpion) foil the antagonist's plot. He is played by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brandon_Terrell&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brandon Terrell (page does not exist)">Brandon Terrell</a> (listed as BT in the credits).</li>
<li><b>Tender Lovin' (Veronica)</b>: Argyle's girlfriend and a former hooker. She and Argyle are constantly arguing about relationship issues. She is played by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Enya_Flack&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Enya Flack (page does not exist)">Enya Flack</a>.</li>
<li><b>Arthur "Artie" Worth</b>: Angel City's extremely corrupt Mayor. Often involved in various shady deals either to fund his campaign for reelection or his extravagant lifestyle. Many criminals in the city are created because of this, including his own <a href="/wiki/Deputy_mayor" title="Deputy mayor">Deputy mayor</a>. He is played by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Pine" title="Robert Pine">Robert Pine</a>.</li>
<li><b>Dr. Phineas Phoenix</b>: A scientist who deals in reanimating the dead, and creating clones. His goal is to take a criminal and reform him into a productive member of society. As a result, several of Black Scorpion's enemies return from fatal defeats at her hand (Breathtaker, Inferno and Gangster Prankster). He is played by <a href="/wiki/Raye_Birk" title="Raye Birk">Raye Birk</a>.</li>
<li><b>Capt. Henry Strickland</b>: Played by <a href="/wiki/Guy_Boyd_(actor)" title="Guy Boyd (actor)">Guy Boyd</a>.</li>
</ul>
