<p>The <b>Westfort Hurricanes</b> were a <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> <a href="/wiki/Junior_ice_hockey" title="Junior ice hockey">Junior</a> <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> club from <a href="/wiki/Fort_William,_Ontario" title="Fort William, Ontario">Fort William</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>. The Herks were members of the <a href="/wiki/Thunder_Bay_Junior_A_Hockey_League" title="Thunder Bay Junior A Hockey League">Thunder Bay Junior A Hockey League</a> and were <a href="/wiki/Abbott_Cup" title="Abbott Cup">Abbott Cup</a> finalists once.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Westfort_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1972, the Hurricanes broke away from the <a href="/wiki/Thunder_Bay_Junior_A_Hockey_League" title="Thunder Bay Junior A Hockey League">Thunder Bay Junior A Hockey League</a> to join the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Paul,_Minnesota" title="St. Paul, Minnesota">St. Paul, Minnesota</a>-based <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Can-Am_Junior_Hockey_League&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Can-Am Junior Hockey League (page does not exist)">Can-Am Junior Hockey League</a>. A year later, that league became the <a href="/wiki/Midwest_Junior_Hockey_League" title="Midwest Junior Hockey League">Midwest Junior Hockey League</a>. The Herks stayed on board for one Midwest Junior season before returning to the TBJHL. The MWJHL later merged and solidified the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Hockey_League" title="United States Hockey League">United States Hockey League</a>.</p>
<p>The Buccaneers folded on July 6, 1980 when the TBAHA left them with no league to play in.</p>
