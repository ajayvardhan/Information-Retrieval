<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A9t%C3%A9o-France&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The organisation was established by decree in June 1993 and is a department of the Ministry of Transportation. It is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> but many domestic operations have been decentralised to <a href="/wiki/Toulouse" title="Toulouse">Toulouse</a>. Its budget of around €300 million is funded by state grants, aeronautic royalties and sale of commercial services.</p>
<p>Météo-France has a particularly strong international presence, and is the French representative at the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>. The organisation is a leading member of <a href="/wiki/EUMETSAT" title="EUMETSAT">EUMETSAT</a>, responsible for the procurement of <a href="/wiki/Meteosat" title="Meteosat">Meteosat</a> weather satellites. It is also member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Institut_au_service_du_spatial,_de_ses_applications_et_technologies" title="Institut au service du spatial, de ses applications et technologies">Institut au service du spatial, de ses applications et technologies</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Worldwide">Worldwide</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A9t%C3%A9o-France&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Worldwide">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to its operations in metropolitan France, the agency provides forecasts and warnings for the French overseas <i>départements</i> and <i>collectivités</i>. It has four sub-divisions based in <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a> (with further divisions serving <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>), <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia" title="New Caledonia">New Caledonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">French Polynesia</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">Réunion</a>. Some of these sub-divisions have particularly important international responsibilities:</p>
<ul>
<li>For example, the <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a> office based at <a href="/wiki/Cayenne" title="Cayenne">Cayenne</a>-Rochambeau airport maintains facilities at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ESA" title="ESA">ESA</a>/<a href="/wiki/CNES" title="CNES">CNES</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Centre_Spatial_Guyanais" title="Centre Spatial Guyanais">Centre Spatial Guyanais</a> <a href="/wiki/Spaceport" title="Spaceport">spaceport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kourou" title="Kourou">Kourou</a> which assists with launch campaigns of the <a href="/wiki/Ariane_(rocket_family)" title="Ariane (rocket family)">Ariane rocket</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">Réunion</a> sub-division is the official <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organisation" title="World Meteorological Organisation">World Meteorological Organisation</a> designated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Centre" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre">Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre</a> (RSMC) for the provision of forecasts and warnings of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones" title="Tropical cyclones">tropical cyclones</a> in the south-west <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">French Polynesia</a> sub-division, whilst not the official RSMC for <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclones</a> in the South Pacific, has been mandated by the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">WMO</a> to issue forecasts and warnings of tropical cyclones for the neighbouring British <a href="/wiki/Pitcairn_Islands" title="Pitcairn Islands">Pitcairn Islands</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A9t%C3%A9o-France&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Naming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the original name of the organization was "<b>Météo-France</b>", with acute accents and normal French capitalization, all the publications made by Météo-France are now using the name written with capitals only, without any accents, everywhere the name is used as a trademark for the products and services delivered by the national organization.</p>
<p>This trademark decision reflects the need to have its name not altered in electronic documents due to transcoding errors, and to allow easier international references in many languages, including when referencing the organization itself (in copyright notices for example, or when citing sources).</p>
<p>The name in capitals or with normal capitalization with accents is protected internationally under trademark law, and as an organization name. Some non-binding information documents sometimes forget the hyphen in the name (but the hyphen is normally required).</p>
