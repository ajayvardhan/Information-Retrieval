<p><b>Aleksandr Yuriyevich "Sasha" Kaleri</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Александр Юрьевич Калери</span>; born <a href="/wiki/J%C5%ABrmala" title="Jūrmala">Jūrmala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>, 13 May 1956) is a Russian cosmonaut and veteran of extended stays on the <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">Mir</a> Space Station and the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> (ISS). Kaleri has most recently been in space aboard the ISS serving as a flight engineer for the long duration <a href="/wiki/Expedition_25" title="Expedition 25">Expedition 25</a>/<a href="/wiki/Expedition_26" title="Expedition 26">26</a> missions. He has spent the third-longest time in space of any person and the longest time in space of any person not born in what is now Russia.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal">Personal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleksandr_Kaleri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kaleri is married to the former Svetlana L. Nosova. They have a son, Oleg Aleksandrovich Kaleri, born in 1996. Kaleri's mother, Antonina Petrovna Kaleri, resides in Sevastopol, Crimea, and his father, Yuri Borisovich Kaleri, is deceased. Kaleri enjoys running, reading and gardening.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleksandr_Kaleri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1979, Kaleri graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Institute_of_Physics_and_Technology" title="Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology">Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dolgoprudny" title="Dolgoprudny">Dolgoprudny</a>, Moscow region, as a specialist in Aircraft Flight Dynamics and Control. In 1983, he completed post-graduate studies at the same institute as a specialist in the field of Mechanics of Fluids and Plasma.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Experience">Experience</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleksandr_Kaleri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Experience">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1979, he was hired by the <a href="/wiki/Energia" title="Energia">Energia</a> Corporation and worked on the <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">Mir</a> space station. He participated in development of design and engineering documentation, full-scale tests of the Mir. Kaleri is a skilled programmer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cosmonaut_career">Cosmonaut career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleksandr_Kaleri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cosmonaut career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Kaleri was selected as the Energia RSC cosmonaut candidate in April 1984. Between 1985 and 1986, he completed basic training and evaluation at the Gagarin Cosmonaut Training Center. In 1987 he was qualified for flight assignment as a test cosmonaut.</p>
<p>Kaleri took a training course for a spaceflight aboard the Mir orbital station as a backup crew flight engineer of the <a href="/wiki/Mir_EO-3" title="Mir EO-3">Mir-3</a> mission from 1 April to 9 December 1987. He took a training course for a spaceflight aboard the Mir as a backup crew flight engineer of the Mir-9 mission from January to April 1991. From 8 October 1991 to 25 February 1992, Kaleri was training as a primary crew flight engineer for the Mir-11 mission.</p>
<p>Kaleri has participated in three extended missions aboard the Mir Space Station. In 1992, he participated in a 145-day flight aboard the Soyuz-TM-14 spacecraft and the Mir. Between 17 August 1996 to 2 March 1997, Kaleri spent 197-days in space aboard the Soyuz-TM-24 spacecraft and the Mir as the Mir-22 mission flight engineer. In 2000, Kaleri performed his third spaceflight aboard the Soyuz-TM-30 spacecraft and the Mir as the Mir-28 mission flight engineer.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Expedition_8">Expedition 8</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleksandr_Kaleri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Expedition 8">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2003, he first flew to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> (ISS) with NASA astronaut <a href="/wiki/Michael_Foale" title="Michael Foale">Michael Foale</a> on his fourth spaceflight. Kaleri served as a Flight Engineer on <a href="/wiki/Expedition_8" title="Expedition 8">Expedition 8</a> and spent 194 days in space from 18 October 2003 to 30 April 2004. The <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-3" title="Soyuz TMA-3">Soyuz TMA-3</a> spacecraft carrying Kaleri, Foale and ESA astronaut <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Kuipers" title="André Kuipers">André Kuipers</a> successfully landed in Kazakhstan at 00:11 GMT on 30 April 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The landing site was located some 60 kilometers northeast of the town of <a href="/wiki/Arkalyk" title="Arkalyk">Arkalyk</a>.</p>
<h3><span id="Expedition_25/26"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Expedition_25.2F26">Expedition 25/26</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleksandr_Kaleri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Expedition 25/26">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kaleri was a member (Flight Engineer) of the ISS Expedition 25/26, that was launched on 7 October 2010 from Baikonur Cosmodrome, aboard <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-01M" title="Soyuz TMA-01M">Soyuz TMA-01M</a> spacecraft, together with cosmonaut <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Skripochka" title="Oleg Skripochka">Oleg Skripochka</a> and NASA astronaut <a href="/wiki/Scott_Kelly_(astronaut)" title="Scott Kelly (astronaut)">Scott Kelly</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spaceflightnow_launch_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-spaceflightnow_launch-4">[4]</a></sup> He served as the Soyuz commander. The mission marked Kaleri's fifth flight into space. He arrived at the ISS after the Soyuz spacecraft linked up with the space station at 00:01 UTC on 10 October 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He remained in the ISS till March 2011. During their mission, Kaleri and the rest of the Expedition 25/26 crew participated in a wide array of research, including fundamental physics, biometric experiments and investigations of crystal growth in space, as well as education outreach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spacewalks">Spacewalks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleksandr_Kaleri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Spacewalks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Kaleri has participated in five spacewalks and has accumulated 23 <a href="/wiki/Extravehicular_activity" title="Extravehicular activity">EVA</a> hours.</p>
<p>On 8 July 1992, at 12:38 UTC Kaleri started his first career spacewalk. During the spacewalk, he and cosmonaut <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Viktorenko" title="Aleksandr Viktorenko">Aleksandr Viktorenko</a> inspected some of Mir's gyrodynes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The spacewalk lasted 2 hours and 3 minutes.</p>
<p>On 2 December 1996, Kaleri and fellow cosmonaut <a href="/wiki/Valery_Korzun" title="Valery Korzun">Valery Korzun</a> completed a 5 hours 57 minutes long spacewalk. The main purpose was to complete connections of the cooperative solar array to provide more electrical power to the Mir station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blaha_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-blaha-8">[8]</a></sup> The spacewalk was Kaleri's second career spacewalk.</p>
<p>On 9 December 1996, Kaleri and fellow cosmonaut Valery Korzun completed a 6 hours 36 minutes long spacewalk. The spacewalk began at 13:50 UTC and ended at 20:28 UTC. During the spacewalk, the two cosmonauts completed connecting the solar array and attached a new Kurs docking antenna subsequently used to guide Progress vehicles docking with Mir. They also repaired the transceiver system that the Mir crew used to talk to amateur operators all around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blaha_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-blaha-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 May 2000, Kaleri and fellow cosmonaut <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Zalyotin" title="Sergei Zalyotin">Sergei Zalyotin</a> completed a five-hour spacewalk to inspect the exterior of Mir. The two spacewalkers also tested a glue which would have been useful to seal tiny cracks, and applied the new airtight sealant to a special panel imitating a damaged section in Mir's skin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The two cosmonauts also examined the exterior of the <a href="/wiki/Progress_M1-2" title="Progress M1-2">Progress M1-2</a> cargo spacecraft.</p>
<p>On 26 February 2004, Kaleri and NASA astronaut <a href="/wiki/Michael_Foale" title="Michael Foale">Michael Foale</a> floated outside the ISS from the <a href="/wiki/Pirs_(ISS_module)" title="Pirs (ISS module)"><i>Pirs</i></a> Docking Compartment airlock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The spacewalk began at 4:17 EST, and Foale and Kaleri were able to complete all but two of their planned tasks, swapping out experiment packages and mounting an instrumented dummy torso on the station's hull to measure the radiation environment faced by spacewalking astronauts. Although the spacewalk was originally planned for 5 hours and 30 minutes, it was cut short due to a cooling system malfunction in Kaleri's spacesuit. Kaleri reported seeing drops of water on the inside of his spacesuit's visor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The spacewalk lasted 3 hours and 55 minutes. The excursion was the fifth career spacewalk for Kaleri.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours_and_awards">Honours and awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleksandr_Kaleri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Honours and awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hero_of_the_Russian_Federation" title="Hero of the Russian Federation">Hero of the Russian Federation</a> (11 August 1992) - for the successful implementation of long-duration space flight on the orbital station <i>Mir</i> and displaying courage and heroism</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Order_of_Merit_for_the_Fatherland" title="Order of Merit for the Fatherland">Order of Merit for the Fatherland</a>;
<ul>
<li>2nd class (9 November 2000) - for courage and heroism displayed during spaceflight on the orbital scientific research complex <i>Mir</i></li>
<li>3rd class (11 April 1997) - for courage and heroism displayed during prolonged space flight 22nd major expedition to the orbital scientific research complex <i>Mir</i></li>
<li>4th class (12 April 2011) - for courage and professionalism shown during the implementation of long-duration space flight on the International Space Station</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Order_of_Friendship" title="Order of Friendship">Order of Friendship</a> (31 October 2005) - for dedication and professionalism shown during the implementation of the 195-day space flight, and the strengthening of friendship between peoples</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Medal_%22For_Merit_in_Space_Exploration%22" title='Medal "For Merit in Space Exploration"'>Medal "For Merit in Space Exploration"</a></li>
